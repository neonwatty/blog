It's tax season. You have a pile of W-2s, 1099s, investment statements, and last year's return open in seventeen tabs. You know AI can help — but you're not about to paste your social security number into a chatbot.

Good news: you don't have to. AI tax preparation works best when you treat it like working with a smart assistant who has no memory and no access to your filing system. You hand them clean, redacted context. They help you figure out what goes where. You stay in control.

Here's how to use AI to file your taxes in 2026.

## Step 1: Collect All Your Documents

Before you touch any AI tool, gather everything in one place. You need the full picture before you start asking questions.

**Income documents:**

- W-2s from every employer
- 1099-NEC or 1099-MISC for freelance/contract income
- 1099-INT for bank interest
- 1099-DIV for dividends
- 1099-B for stock/crypto sales
- K-1s if you're part of a partnership or S-corp

**Deduction and credit documents:**

- 1098 for mortgage interest
- 1098-T for tuition
- Property tax statements
- Charitable donation receipts
- Health insurance (1095-A/B/C)
- Childcare expenses (if applicable)

**Context from last year:**

- Your 2024 tax return (1040 + all schedules you filed)
- Any carryforward amounts (capital losses, etc.)

Put them all in a single folder. PDF is ideal — most of these come as PDFs anyway.

## Step 2: Redact Sensitive Information

This is the step most "use AI for taxes" guides skip. Don't skip it.

AI chatbots like Claude and ChatGPT process your input on remote servers. Even with strong privacy policies, there's no reason to send your social security number, bank account numbers, or full legal name through a chat interface when you don't need to.

**What to redact:**

- Social security numbers
- Bank account and routing numbers
- Full names (replace with initials or "Taxpayer 1" / "Taxpayer 2")
- Full addresses (keep the state for tax residency purposes)
- Employer EINs (optional — low risk, but not needed for most questions)

**How to redact on macOS:** Open each PDF in Preview, use the rectangle annotation tool, set the fill to black, and drag it over the sensitive fields. It takes about 30 seconds per document.

![Example of a redacted tax form using macOS Preview — name and SSN blacked out](/images/posts/how-to-file-taxes-with-ai-2026/redacted-tax-form-preview.png)

The result doesn't need to be forensically perfect. You're not publishing these — you're just keeping PII out of your chat history. The numbers that matter for tax calculation (income amounts, withholding, deductions) stay visible.

## Step 3: Clean Up the Noise

Tax documents are full of filler that wastes AI context. Before you paste or upload anything, strip it down to the signal.

**Remove or skip:**

- "This page intentionally left blank"
- Multi-page fine print and legal disclosures
- Instructions pages (the AI already knows tax rules better than those inserts)
- Duplicate copies (you don't need Copy B, Copy C, and Copy 2 of the same W-2)
- Header/footer boilerplate that repeats on every page

**Keep:**

- All dollar amounts and box numbers
- Form numbers and tax year
- Any state-specific forms
- Notes about carryforwards or amended returns

If you're working with PDFs, delete the junk pages in Preview before uploading to your AI tool. For last year's return, the key pages are your 1040, any schedules (A, B, C, D, SE, etc.), and the summary — not the 20 pages of instructions the IRS stapled on.

## Step 4: Set Up Your Tax App and Start Filing with AI

Now for the actual filing. Here's the workflow:

1. **Open your tax software.** FreeTaxUSA, TurboTax, H&R Block — whatever you use. Start a new return and get to the first input screen.

2. **Open Claude or ChatGPT in a separate window.** Upload your redacted documents (or paste the relevant numbers). Start with something like:

   > Here are my tax documents for 2025. I'm using [tax software] to file. Walk me through what I need to enter, form by form. Ask me for any information you need.

3. **Work through it section by section.** The AI will map your documents to the fields in your tax software. When your software asks "Did you receive any 1099-INT forms?" — you already know the answer because you collected everything in Step 1, and the AI can tell you exactly what numbers to enter where.

4. **Use AI for the confusing parts.** The real value isn't data entry — it's the questions you'd normally Google. "Do I need Schedule SE?" "Can I deduct my home office if I'm W-2?" "What's the difference between standard and itemized for my situation?" These are the questions where AI tax preparation genuinely saves hours.

5. **Review before you file.** Have the AI double-check your return against your source documents. Ask it to verify that every income document is accounted for and that your total income, deductions, and credits look reasonable.

## What AI Can and Can't Do for Taxes

**AI is great at:**

- Explaining which forms and schedules you need
- Mapping box numbers on your documents to fields in tax software
- Answering "should I itemize or take the standard deduction" with your actual numbers
- Catching things you might miss ("you have capital losses you can carry forward")
- Explaining tax concepts in plain English

**AI is not a replacement for:**

- A CPA, if you have a genuinely complex situation (business ownership, multi-state, international income)
- Actually filing — you still need tax software or a preparer to submit
- Legal tax advice — AI can explain the rules, but it's not your accountant

For most people with W-2 income, a few 1099s, and standard deductions, this workflow handles 90% of the thinking. The 10% that's left is the stuff that's always been worth a professional's time.

## The Privacy-First Approach

The whole point of the redaction step is that you get AI tax help without the privacy tradeoff. You're not uploading raw tax documents to a cloud service. You're not pasting your SSN into a chat window. You're giving the AI exactly the context it needs — dollar amounts, form numbers, and your questions — and keeping everything else local.

Your taxes are one of the most sensitive documents you produce every year. Treat them that way, and AI becomes a tool you can actually trust with the process.
